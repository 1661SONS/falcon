WHOLE HEALTH.

Going mobile with healthcare that you manage, while eliminating the emotional vaccuum between you and your doctor..

Channeling Aladdin: "Do you trust me?"
  • Concept: Whole Health by WebMD / Date: Summer 2014. / Category: Mobile, Trust

The foundation for the success of this project rested on coming up with an intuitive way to incentivize users to trust Whole Health with their most private information; their medical records. In short, how we did this can be summarized in one phrase: 'be conversational'. It was apparent how much users trusted WebMD to relay symptoms, health information, and even drug facts, but it was not apparent why this trust was present. How could we replicate WebMD's impressive web following in a mobile app? We answered this with another phrase: "be transparent".

WebMD's online symptom checker and research repository has no problem making you feel like there is a fine line between a headache and brain cancer, nor does it sugarcoat how certain pharmaceutical drugs can cause both. Yet, we needed to take advantage of these attributes without scaring people. The symptom checker was chosen as the prime instrument to gently nudge users for pertinent medical information, while assuring them that having such information would make the app extremely advantageous for managing their health.

Pulling data from other apps, such as Nike+, FitBit, Weight Watchers, heart rate readers, etc., along with having immediate access to a patient's medical history, chronic disease information, current medications, etc., it can be understood why we named it Whole Health.. It can also be understood why we had to constantly check over our shoulders for the Scope Creep. The most difficult part of this project was constantly keeping the Creep at bay. The takeaway here was that more features does not necessarily mean more helpful.
